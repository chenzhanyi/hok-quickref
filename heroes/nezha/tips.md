# Nezha Tips & Tricks

---

## Core Gameplay Tips

### 1. S3: Don't "Fly to Your Death"

New Nezhas see a low-HP enemy and immediately fly. Result: land into 5 enemies and die. The correct S3 usage:
- **Split-push 4-1:** Push side lane → teamfight breaks → S3 onto enemy backline (not the tank)
- **Vision denial:** S3 reveals all enemies + cancels their recalls. Use before Dragon/Baron fights even if you don't fly
- **Cleanup:** Only fly when your team is already engaged or you're cleaning up a won fight

### 2. S2: Never Double-Cast Instantly

S2 has 2 charges. Each grants **3 seconds of 15-20% DR**. The mistake: burning both charges instantly wastes 3+ seconds of free damage reduction. Correct: S2 → wait 3s → S2 again.

### 3. S1: Max Movement Speed Stacks

Each enemy Hero hit by S1 grants a MS stack (max 5). In lane, fight inside minion waves. In teamfights, aim S1 into the densest area.

### 4. Anti-Heal Passive = Counter-Pick Value

Nezha's passive applies **15% Healing Reduction**. This stacks with items for even more anti-heal. He's the natural counter to Cheng Yaojin, Cai Yan, and lifesteal-reliant ADCs.

### 5. Split-Push Is Your Identity

Nezha can ALWAYS join fights thanks to S3. Abuse this: push the farthest lane from objectives. When enemies respond, your team takes Dragon. When they ignore you, you take towers.

## Signature Combos

### Global Backline Delete: **S3 → AA → S1 → S2 → AA → S2**

1. S3 onto enemy squishy (Erin, Hou Yi)
2. AA on landing (trigger on-hit effects)
3. S1 — max MS stacks from nearby enemies
4. S2 first charge → DR + reposition
5. AA weave
6. S2 second charge → seal their escape

### Lane Trade: **S2 → S1 → AA → S2 (retreat)**

1. S2 first charge in → DR activates
2. S1 + AA for damage
3. S2 second charge back to safety before DR expires
4. True Damage burn continues ticking while you disengage

## Advanced Tips

- **S3 grants Tenacity on landing.** Combined with Resistance Boots, you cap Tenacity and ignore most CC.
- **Don't always fly.** The THREAT of S3 changes enemy behavior. Sometimes just revealing them is enough.
- **Matchup:** Dominates sustain-based comps. Struggles vs burst-heavy teams that delete you before DR matters.
